### New handlers to add (Step 9d)
1. `allowListGuardian_addSolver_asGuardian(address)` — GUARDIAN_ROLE → Guardian.addSolver (always reverts at onlyTimelock; sets ghost if it ever doesn't). Feeds SP-17.
2. `allowListGuardian_setManager_asGuardian(address)` — GUARDIAN_ROLE → Guardian.setManager (always reverts). Feeds SP-17.
3. `allowListGuardian_setGuardian_asGuardian(address)` — GUARDIAN_ROLE → Guardian.setGuardian, incl. self-target (always reverts). Feeds SP-17, SP-18.
4. `allowListGuardian_removeSolver_asTimelock(address)` — TIMELOCK_ROLE → Guardian.removeSolver (always reverts at onlyGuardian). Feeds SP-17.
5. `allowListGuardian_addSolver_backToBack(address)` — two `allowListGuardian.addSolver` calls as TIMELOCK_ROLE in the same block (no vm.roll/warp between). Feeds SP-20.
6. `gPv2AllowListAuthentication_managerRoundTrip(uint256 bSeed)` — bespoke: read `m0=manager()`; pick actor `b != m0`; `vm.prank(m0) proposeManager(b)`; `vm.prank(b) acceptManagership()`; `vm.prank(b) proposeManager(m0)`; `vm.prank(m0) acceptManagership()`; assert `manager()==m0 && pendingManager()==0`. Feeds SP-04. (All addresses are prankable, incl. the Guardian contract address and role addresses.)

harness bug. Do NOT soften them into always-true no-ops. If the campaign fires one, Step 10 triage
records the call sequence and flags it EXPLORATORY (lead), not a confirmed bug.
