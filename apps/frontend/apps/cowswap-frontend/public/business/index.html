Why Ophis

Three things desks consistently care about.

These are mechanism-level properties, not marketing slogans. Each is implemented at the contract or settlement layer Ophis inherits from CoW Protocol.

Non-custodial by construction

Your tokens never leave your wallet until on-chain settlement. The operator cannot move, freeze, or recover funds. No counterparty custody risk; no recoverable hot wallet.

Operational boundaries

What Ophis is — and isn't.

Explicit scope statements so there are no surprises during integration discussions.

What Ophis is

A non-custodial interface to the CoW Protocol settlement layer, with a plain-English intent parser, partner-fee mechanism (CIP-75), and cross-chain destination support via NEAR Intents. Operated by a Luxembourg-incorporated consultancy. Open-source under its own license.

What Ophis isn't

A regulated investment firm; a custodian; a guaranteed-execution venue; an ISO-certified provider; an exchange or market maker. Execution depends on solver competition and upstream protocol availability. We do not promise outcomes we cannot enforce on-chain.

FAQ

Questions desks usually ask first.

Are you a regulated financial-services entity?

No. The operator is a Luxembourg-incorporated consultancy company providing a non-custodial software interface to permissionless on-chain protocols. Ophis does not take custody of funds, execute discretionary trades, or hold a regulated financial-services license. For formal contractual arrangements, full entity details are provided on request via the contact below.

What is your fee structure?

0% on ordinary trades. Ophis charges nothing on the principal value of a swap. For trades where the execution price improves on your quote, Ophis may receive 25% of the verified price improvement — capped at 0.5% of the trade volume — via the CIP-75 partner-fee mechanism inherited from CoW Protocol.

No hidden spreads, no surprise basis points. Partner-tier fee discounts may apply under formal terms (see the engagement tiers above).

Can I get a custom rate limit?

Yes — partner-tier rate limits are reviewed case-by-case. The public Intent API allows 30 requests/min/IP, which is adequate for low/medium-volume operators. For higher throughput, reach out with a brief description of your use case + target volume and we'll respond usually within one business day.

How do I integrate?

Three paths (more detail above): (1) point your operators at ophis.fi for direct use, (2) call POST /api/intent from your bot/agent, or (3) embed the upstream CoW Widget. Documentation at ophis.fi/docs.

What happens if a solver fails or upstream protocol has an outage?

Solver competition handles single-solver failures automatically — your order is settled by whichever solver wins the batch. If the underlying CoW Protocol orderbook is degraded (rare but possible), Ophis surfaces an explicit error instead of routing through a degraded venue. Unsettled orders incur no Ophis or CoW settlement fee — though network gas costs may still apply to any token approvals or other wallet actions you signed before the order was placed.

Do you have references or case studies?

Not yet — Ophis is pre-launch in commercial terms. We list specific partner integrations here only after a signed agreement exists; we don't publish anonymous "trusted by" claims. If you'd like to be an early reference partner, mention it in your first email.
